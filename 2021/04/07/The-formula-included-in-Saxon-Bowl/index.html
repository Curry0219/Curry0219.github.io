The formula included in Saxon Bowl

Posted on 2021-04-07

Several weeks later, I’m here again to upload my project about Saxon Bowl, the Progress is in the stage of 3D printing and coding, hence there is not much valuable things to upload here, hence I plan to upload The Progress in summer holiday as it has its primary shape and waiting to be programmed.

In the last post I mentioned the rough procedures to do experiments and proof that the bowl is sinking with a constant velocity in water, and this time, I want to discuss the physical principles deeper and in order to determine how the Bernoulli’s principle can be applied in Saxon Bowl experiments.

The Bernoulli’s principle can be get from the principle of the conservation of energy , which is

½ mv²+mgh=constant , that means the kinetic energy and the gravitational energy always transfer to each other in a close system with no extra force. According to this formula, we divide both side of the formula by V (volume) to get that P+½ ρv²+ρgh=constant , this formula means that the pressure of the fluid, the sum of the gravitational potential pressure of the fluid and the kinetic energy is a constant. I our daily life, if there is a tube, and which is filled with water and the water is flowing through the tube with constant speed (there is force exert on both end of the tube ), at a moment if we open a hole at the top of the tube, the pressure of the fluid, which is air, will be lower and hence the water will be pumped out through the hole as the kinetic energy of the water is transferred to gravitational energy. In the formula of the Bernoulli’s principle, the P is the pressure that a force act on a particular area, ½ ρv² is the kinetic energy divided by velocity and ρgh is the pressure of liquid because of the height of the liquid.

Moreover, we can usually observe a phenomenon that the water column will be thinner as it falls down, which can be described by Bernoulli’s principle as well. As the water is falling, the g.p.e turns to k.e, but as the whole sum of k.e, g.p.e and P is constant, the system will make P constant as well, but F is increasing because the water upward is more and more, hence the cross sectional area will be smaller hence the water becomes thinner.

I another example, if there is a special tube, the width of f=the both end of the tube is same and the median part are thinner, in this case, the speed of the water in the medium part of the tube is higher. It is a little bit complex to explain, the thinner part of the tube will give an opposite force to the water in the medium part of the tube, and the number of water molecules will decrease. As the water molecules in the front side of the tube is moving, it experiences an opposite force, but the water behind it move forward with constant velocity, at this time the water in front part will experience an acceleration which is caused by the force given by the molecules behind, the pressure is constant but the cross sectional area decreases, hence the force increases. And hence the velocity of the water increases. So in conclusion, each part of the water in a tube will give a pressure to the water in front of it, and they are used to overcome the resistance of the water and increase the speed of the water in the next part.
