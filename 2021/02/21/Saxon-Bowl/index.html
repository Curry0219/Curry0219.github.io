Saxon Bowl

Posted on 2021-02-21

During the winter holiday, my friend Jasper invited me to research the principle and function of a amazing physical object called Saxon Bowl. Which was an object used by ancient people in a particular region to measure and record the time passing.

To make sure our plan can be practicable and we can manage to complete our research, we invited a professor to help us. In the first week, we watch the university in China make open reply which is relative to our research target.

After our discussion, we decided to use mathematical and physical knowledge to predict the result with formulas. And here is our process.

As the picture shows, we firstly determine the graph of the relationship between time and the mass of water which is taken by the volume of the bowl. There is mostly 3 conditions. The first one is that there is no hole on the bottom of the bowl. Hence according to the gravity, and the average density of the bowl is bigger than water,it sinks slowly with a decreasing acceleration and finally comes to the equilibrium, in this condition the upthrust force is equal to the gravity. Hence M(water) = M(object) . But it is hard to prduce a bowl whose average density is bigger than water, hence in the experiments later, all of our bowl firstly fluctuates and float on the surface of the water.

The second condition is that the bowl has no bottom, which means that the diameter of the hole is equal to the diameter of the bowl. Hence the bowl will sink extremely fast also with a decreasing acceleration.

Finally, the condition that there is a small hole at the bottom of the bowl, because of that, there will be some water enter the bowl and hence there will be increasing mass of the whole bowl and the gravity acts on the bowl is also increasing. From the graph we made, we can obtain that the mass of the water taken by the bowl is almost constant because as the bowl is sinking and there is some water enter the bowl as well, but because of the difference between the holes, the velocity and rate of water enter the bowl is different, hence the graph is fluctuating.

We deduce the result by firstly think that there must be a moment at which the upthrust force iis equal to the gravity, hence we can write F(P)=F(G) and we change these formulae into other formulae, hence mg =ρgπR2·Y1 (1). In which ρ is the density of the water and Y1 is the height of the water which is taken place, we can apparently obtain these relationships in this graph.

Further, because of the bowl is sinking, when the bowl sinks downward for ΔY, the surface of the liquid inside the bowl rises for Δy, hence ∆Fp= ρgπR^2 ∆Y and ∆N = ρgπ(R-δ1 )^2 ∆y this is the formula which shows the change of the gravity of the liquid inside the bowl and δ1 is the thickness of the bowl. And because of the conservation of mass, the total mass is always constant hence ∆Fp=∆N and hence

ρπR^2 ∆Y = ρπ(R-δ_1 )^2 ∆y, in which ∆Y is the difference of the surface of the liquid outside the bowl, and ∆y is the the difference of the surface of the liquid inside the bowl. Finally we combine the formulae and get

mg+ρπR^2 ∆Y=ρgπR2·Y_1+ρπ(R-δ_1 )^2 ∆y

According to the formulae we can know that the bowl is sinking with constant velocity.
