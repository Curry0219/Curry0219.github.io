the monopole motor

Posted on 2021-09-11

These days I was boosting the Progress, and I am going to update my process in the last blog, and there are a lot of things worth to talk about.

And now I want to say something about the electromagnetic.

As I learned in the A-level physics, there is plenty of principles about the electromagnetic, and one of them is “Fleming Left Hand Rule”, which states that if we put the fingers of our left hand as a coordinate system. The middle finger represents the direction of the current, the index finger represents for the direction of the magnetic field and the thumb represents the direction of the force.

And according to this I came up with an idea, as I have learned a little electrical machinery, I know the basic principles that how a motor can start to spin, which is mainly based on the Fleming Left Hand rule, and at that time I started to think, since the principle is so easy to understand, there must be a kind of simplest motor just made by a magnet with current through it. And since then I started to do the research online, and accidently, when I watch the video of the electroBOOM in YouTube, I learned there is a motor indeed, which is called the monopile motor, just like what I thought, it is all base on the Fleming’s left hand rule. After watching the video, I decided to make a monopole motor by myself.

Hence I drew the circuit of the device rapidly, as shown.

And it is quite obvious, according to the rule, if now the battery offers current in the direction of the green arrow, and the magnetic field is shown as the red arrows, the conductor, which is a wire will be exerted a force outward the paper.

The theory is reasonable, and now I should do the practice experiments. Hence as the picture shows, I installed a device like this.
