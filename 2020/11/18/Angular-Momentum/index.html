Angular Momentum!!!

When I was still a little boy, I was interest in weapons like guns, bullets and missiles. And I had a question that is if I have a gun and shoot a bullet right above my head and vertical to the place I stand on, will the bullet hit my head and kill me as it falls?

After learning inertance when I was in grade 8, I thought the bullet will hit me because of the inertance.

But my opinion at that time seems ridiculous right now. Because there is the law of conservation of angular momentum.

What is the conservation of angular momentum??

When an object is spinning, it is making circular motion. In physics, we can calculate the linear velocity by multiply the angular velocity with radius. Which means in a moment, the velocity of a point moving in the orbital the circle.

According to this diagram, we can observe that p is the linear velocity and r is the radius of the circle. Hence the angular momentum L=rp. The principle of the conservation of the angular momentum is that to point O, it experiences the resultant moment force of 0 and the angular momentum of this point will not change.

So in the formula, the L does not change at all, at this time, we change the value of r, if we increase the radius of the circle, the v will decrease in order to make sure that the L does not change.

So it is quite easy to explain that why skating athletes can spin for a long time without stopping. Because if we make the body as the axe and the whole people is making a circular motion and the athletes always extent their arms and bend their arms. They bend their arms hence the radius decreases and the v increases so they can spin faster.

Amazing!!!!
