What is Wabi Sabi

Wabi sabi

In traditional Japanese aesthetics, wabi-sabi is a world view centered on the acceptance of transience and imperfection. The aesthetic is sometimes described as one of beauty that is “imperfect, impermanent, and incomplete”. It is a concept derived from the Buddhist teaching of the three marks of existence(sanbōin), specifically impermanence(mujō), suffering(ku) and emptiness or absence of self-nature (kū).

Characteristics of the wabi-sabi aesthetic include asymmetry, roughness, simplicity, economy, austerity, modesty, intimacy, and appreciation of the ingenuous integrity of natural objects and processes.

It is also a form of art which can be described as “the most conspicuous and characteristic feature” of what we think of traditional Japanese beauty, said Leonard Koren.

As the main principle of wabi sabi is imperfect and incomplete, we can simply observe the difference by comparing the tea vessel made in modern style and in wabi sabi style.

In contrast, we can clearly see the difference between normal style and the wabi sabi style, in the Japanese tea ceremony, the pottery items used are often rustic and simple-looking, e.g. Hagi ware, with shapes that are not quite symmetrical, and colors or textures that appear to emphasize an unrefined or simple style. Moreover, wabi sabi use plenty of black, white and gray tinge to create its particular atmosphere. It also a historical form of art, many Japanese arts over the past thousand years have been influenced by Zen and Mahayana philosophy, particularly acceptance and contemplation of the imperfection, constant flux and impermanence of all things. Such arts can exemplify a wabi-sabi aesthetic.

The wabi sabi lifestyle

Since imperfection is the basic principle of Wabi-Sabi, the Japanese philosophy of accepting our making the most of life.

As I’ve mentioned, “Wabi” is said to be defined as “rustic simplicity” or “understated elegance” with a focus on a less-is-more mentality while “Sabi” is translated to “taking pleasure in the imperfect.” The Japanese philosophy encourages us to focus on the blessings hiding in our daily lives, and celebrating the way things are rather than how they should be. So the wabi sabi lifestyle is not only a style of art or decoration, it is more likely a life style and the attitude to face the whole world.

Another reason why wabi sabi life style is more and more popular is that it tend to use natural materials, this is closely connect to the earth hence it uses more natural materials.

There is no need to say more about its philosophy of simple, with wabi sabi, simplicity is the key. The freshome website says that we need to allow our sourced pieces space to shine on their own and, whenever possible, to bring an element of nature into the room.
